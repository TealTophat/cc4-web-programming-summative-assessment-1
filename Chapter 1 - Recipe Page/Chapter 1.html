Beef Nilaga - A Recipe by Vanjo Merano

This is one of my first experiences in cooking, and is still a dish I love to make and eat. For a little bit of context, Beef Nilaga (or Nilagang Baka) is a Filipino dish that is simple to prepare, and can accomodate a good amount of people. There are other varieties of this recipe, but this recipe is what I usually use. Additionally, this recipe was originally by Vanjo Merano, a man who also has a popular cooking channel known as "Panlasang Pinoy."


If you want to see the original recipe, click here. There's also a video!

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 Ibs. beef brisket, cubed
  • 1 big potato, cubed
  • 1 bok choy or pechay
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 medium-sized carrots, chopped crosswise with a length of ~2 in.
  • 1 tbsp whole pepper corn
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce (patis)

HOW TO COOK IT

  1. Saute the onion until texture becomes soft
  2. Add-in the beef brisket and cook for about 5 minutes
  3. Add the fish sauce and whole pepper corn then mix well
  4. Pour the water and bring to a boil. Simmer for 1 to 2 hours (or 30 minutes if using pressure cooker)
  5. Put-in the vegetables starting with the potato and the carrots. Simmer for 5 to 7 minutes
  6. Add the bok choy (Pechay). Cover the pot and turn off the heat. Let it stay for 5 minutes.
  7. Serve hot. Share and Enjoy!
